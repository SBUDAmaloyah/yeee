About the Author


Mbulelo E. Mandindi

I was born in South Africa, Dube Village, in the massive township of Soweto, Johannesburg, which is known as the City of gold. My mother was Xhosa, and father a Zulu. I left the township at the age of five and went to an obscure rural area in the province of the Eastern Cape where I grew up as a shepherd boy, looking after my auntâ€™s livestock. Although a backwater, I believe it helped me understand nature and life; the silence allowed me to contemplate.

Growing up in the villages, entertainment was limited. But there was boxing, rugby, and football, which we loved. The Eastern Cape is the South African home of Boxing.

After finishing matric, I went to stay in Cape Town which I loved as a young adult, but as time passed my stay in Cape Town became difficult. Living in the townships (Langa and Gugulethu) was harsh.
